Chicken Marsala

üçó Description

This is a classic Italian-style dish featuring tender, thin-cut chicken breasts pan-fried until lightly golden, then simmered with mushrooms in a rich, slightly sweet Marsala wine sauce. It's elegant enough for dinner guests yet easy enough for a weekday meal.

Ingredients

  • Boneless, skinless chicken breast halves (pounded thin)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • Dried oregano
  • Butter
  • Olive oil
  • Sliced fresh mushrooms
  • Marsala wine
  • Cooking sherry (optional, per recipe variation)
  • Chicken stock or broth

Steps

  1. In a shallow dish, whisk together flour, salt, pepper, and dried oregano.
  2. Dredge the chicken pieces in the seasoned flour mixture, shaking off excess.
  3. Heat olive oil and butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
  4. Cook the chicken until lightly browned on both sides. Remove and set aside.
  5. Add mushrooms to the skillet and saut√© until softened.
  6. Pour in Marsala wine (and sherry if used), scraping the pan to release browned bits.
  7. Return the chicken to the skillet, cover, and simmer for about 10 minutes, flipping once, until fully cooked through.
  8. When juices run clear and chicken is no longer pink, serve hot with the sauce spooned over top.
